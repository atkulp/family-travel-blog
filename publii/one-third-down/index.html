By Arian and Karen Published on December 1, 2019

One-third down!

As I type this, it’s Sunday evening. It’s been a sweltering hot last few days with temperatures up into the 90’s, with some areas (what they call the lowveld) over 100℉! The next few days promise more heat until Wednesday when it suddenly drops a ridiculous 40°. We are expecting some rough weather, likely hailstorms again. Just when we thought we were done with hail!

Since we started August 1*

Last week, we spent a few days at the Peace Corps training campus (actually local school that lets them use it) for Thanksgiving. Volunteers don’t often get together formally, other than sometimes for the swearing-in of new groups, so some of the time was mandatory. We had some talks about being effective, new or revised policies and procedures, and some peer sessions where people talked about their projects or lessons learned. On Thursday, we spent about half the day at the country director’s house. It was a beautiful home and there was an enormous spread of food! The whole time was great for reconnecting with people, making new friends, and strengthening ties.

In just over two weeks, we’ll be going to Japan to meet Damian and Lilly, and a few days later, Gabriel and Kendrea will be arriving! G&K will be finished with their semester abroad and heading home. We’re so excited that we can all meet up in the same place! We’ve been having weekly video calls, but it’s never the same. Often, the bandwidth here or in Nepal just isn’t conducive to a good connection.

This week should be a good one. We are preparing to put out our second software release, and drawing up plans for the post-JAD updates. It’s a lot of work, and no small amount of pressure to get things right. We are looking forward to the challenge though!
