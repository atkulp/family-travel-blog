Glossary

Places

    • Boudhanath (Boudha) – The Tibetan district of Kathmandu. Home of our host family and many monasteries, including Ka-Nying Shedrub Ling. Famous for the Boudhanath Stupa.

    • Himalayas – The mountains that form the north border of Nepal.

    • Ka-Nying Shedrub Ling – The monastery that hosts RYI.

    • Kathmandu – The capital city of Nepal. The elevation is a bit lower than that of Denver, CO.

    • Oregon State University (OSU) – Our home university.

    • Rangjung Yeshe Institute (RYI) – The international school that operates within the monastery. RYI is associated with Kathmandu University, which provides credit for our classes.

    • Tibet – A mountainous region in western China, separated from Nepal by the Himalayas. The Chinese government suppresses the traditional Buddhist, nomadic culture, so many Tibetans have moved elsewhere — many to Nepal — to practice their lifestyle in peace.

People

Teachers

    • Chökyi Nyima Rinpoche – The spiritual head of Ka-Nying Shedrub Ling. “World-renowned teacher and meditation master in the Kagyü and Nyingma traditions of Tibetan Buddhism.”

    • Geoff Barstow (Dr. Barstow) – The Oregon State University professor who organizes this study abroad. He studies vegetarianism in Tibetan Buddhism.

    • Fr. Greg Sharkey – A Catholic priest, professor, and our academic advisor. He is also a key part of our safety net, keeping tabs on everyone and reporting potential issues to Dr. Barstow.

Religious Terms

    • Brahmin — The “priestly” caste; the highest social level in Hinduism, which requires special care to maintain ritual purity; the caste of our host family and their servants.

    • Buddhism – A minority religion in Nepal, and the main object of our focus for this trip.

    • Hinduism – The majority religion in Nepal and India.

    • Lama – A Tibetan Buddhist spiritual leader.

    • Khenpo/Khenmo – Someone who has earned the Tibetan Buddhist equivalent of a doctorate.

    • Stupa – A Buddhist landmark for worship. Boudhanath has the largest stupa in Nepal.

    • Tibetan Buddhism – A type of Mahayana Buddhism developed in Tibet and associated with Vajrayana and Tantra. Its four major schools are Kagyü, Nyingma, Sakya, and Gelug; the “Ka-Nying” in “Ka-Nying Shedrub Ling” is short for “Kagyü and Nyingma”.
